"20 Sites in 40 Nights"

I have been fortunate enough to have volunteered at Loaves and Fishes for a long time. However, being the innovators that they are, Loaves and Fishes has expanded greatly, and adapted their business model to continue to meet the needs of their Guests. So, in an effort to better understand the "new" Loaves and Fishes, I am attempting to help out at a lot of sites over the next month or so. I'm calling it "20 sites in 40 nights". Please read along with my posts as I make my way around the Twin Cities. Additionally, if you are so compelled, please visit this link to either volunteer your time, or donate some money. Every $10 donated can feed a family of four! Thanks, and we’ll see you Monday at site #1!

Guess what color I’ll be seeing in my dreams tonight? That’s right, I cut two crates of oranges at the Holy Rosary site. They were served as part of a delicious, nutritious meal served to over 250 hungry guests that waited patiently for their takeaway dinners. Most of the guests live down the street as part of the Little Earth Residence Association. I worked alongside six amazing, dedicated volunteers who have spent the first Monday of every month for the last 40 years, serving meals in this South Minneapolis community.

My second site visit brought me to Crystal, MN. Crystal is a beautiful suburb of Minneapolis, once again proving that the need is everywhere. I helped out the kind volunteers from Valley of the Peace, as they served meals at one of L&F’s Twin Cities sites - Brunswick United Methodist Church. The site coordinator, Angela, put together an amazing meal for 60 hungry and thankful guests Admirably, she is a “floater” for L&F, meaning she goes to whichever sites she is needed that day. The food was delivered to the site earlier in the day by the Commissary Kitchen - a central spot for gathering, assembling, and disseminating nutritious meals.

Bad weather doesn't stop mail carriers, and it evidently doesn't stop Loaves and Fishes, either. The snow was flying on Saturday, but hungry guests still lined up, happy to take hot meals home to their families on a blustery winter's night. Last night's meal was the most delicious yet. Provided by India Palace of Eden Prairie, this meal of rice, beans, naan, and apple slices was absolutely amazing. Our leader, Robin, directed me and the other volunteers (the Sikh Society) in plating and serving over 90 guests. Members of the Sikh Society show up to this facility every month (the 2nd Saturday) and happily feed all that make the trek to this Richfield site.
